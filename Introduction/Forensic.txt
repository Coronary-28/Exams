Which of the following is NOT an early sign of death?
A) Livor mortis (postmortem hypostasis)
B) algor mortis (Cooling of the body)
C) Pallor mortis
D) Rigor mortis
E) Loss of peripheral pulse
Correct Answer: E) Loss of peripheral pulse
Explanation:
Livor mortis, algor mortis, pallor mortis, and rigor mortis are all recognized early postmortem changes that develop progressively after death and are used forensically to help diagnose and estimate time of death. Loss of peripheral pulse occurs immediately at the moment of cardiac arrest itself, making it a clinical sign confirming death rather than a postmortem change that develops afterward.
###
Traumatic asphyxia results from severe injury to which part of the body?
A) Neck
B) Chest (with abdomen)
C) Abdomen only
D) Mouth and nose
E) Larynx
Correct Answer: B) Chest (with abdomen)
Explanation:
Traumatic asphyxia is defined as compression on the chest and abdomen by external force, impairing the mechanical ability to breathe. This differs from strangulation/hanging (neck level) and smothering (mouth and nose level), which are other forms of violent asphyxia acting at different anatomical levels.
###
In forensic medicine, strangulation can be categorized into which manner of death?
A) Suicidal manner
B) Homicidal manner
C) Accidental manner
D) Natural manner
E) Undetermined manner
Correct Answer: B) Homicidal manner
Explanation:
Strangulation (compression of neck structures by ligature or hands) is mostly homicidal, unlike hanging, which is mostly suicidal but can be accidental or homicidal. This distinction in manner of death is forensically important because it directly affects legal classification and investigation of the case.
###
Which type of injury is not caused by a blunt object but rather by a sharp object, typically resulting in clean, well-defined wound edges?
A) Laceration
B) Abrasion
C) Contusion
D) Incised (incision) wound
E) Stab wound
Correct Answer: D) Incised (incision) wound
Explanation:
Incisions/cut wounds are caused by a clean, sharp-edged object such as a knife or glass splinter, producing regular edges, clear margins, and no tissue bridging. In contrast, lacerations, abrasions, and contusions all result from blunt trauma and typically show irregular edges, contused margins, and tissue bridging.
###
What is the approximate time window during which myocardial cells can be harvested after cardiac arrest?
A) 60 minutes
B) 5-7 minutes
C) 45 minutes
D) 2 hours
E) 24 hours
Correct Answer: A) 60 minutes
Explanation:
Different tissues and organs remain viable for different durations after death, since cells do not die instantly and organs vary in their tolerance to anoxia (the lecture notes the brain survives only 5-7 minutes and the kidney about 45 minutes). This variability explains why timing is critical for organ/tissue donation and harvesting after cardiac arrest.
###
Stab wounds can be classified into which of the following categories?
A) Sharp
B) Blunt
C) All of the above
D) Blunt with a pointed ending
E) None of the above
Correct Answer: C) All of the above
Explanation:
Stab wounds can result from a purely sharp instrument, a blunt instrument, or a blunt object with a pointed tip (as illustrated by the example of a worker falling onto a round-tipped iron rod), since what matters legally and clinically is the depth and mechanism of penetration rather than the object's overall sharpness. This is why all three categories are considered valid classifications of stab wounds in forensic assessment.
###
Which of the following is NOT a cause of an incisional (cut) wound?
A) Knife
B) Razor
C) Broken glass
D) Rock
E) Glass splinter
Correct Answer: D) Rock
Explanation:
Incisions/cut wounds are caused by a clean, sharp-edged object such as a knife, razor, or glass splinter/broken glass. A rock is a blunt object, so injury from it produces a laceration (irregular tear-like wound), not an incision — making it the exception among the choices.
###
The mechanism of rigor mortis involves decreased ATP leading to which direct biochemical event in muscle cells?
A) Depolymerization of actin filaments
B) Binding of actin and myosin fibres into a gel
C) Breakdown of myosin heads
D) Calcium efflux from the sarcoplasmic reticulum
E) Increased ATP-dependent cross-bridge cycling
Correct Answer: B) Binding of actin and myosin fibres into a gel
Explanation:
After death, anaerobic respiration causes lactic acid buildup and increasing acidity; combined with low ATP, this causes the actin and myosin fibres to bind together and form a stiff gel, producing rigor mortis. This is why rigor mortis preserves and reflects the position the person died in, rather than reflecting later body movement.
###
Smothering is best defined as which of the following?
A) Compression on neck structures by ligature
B) Compression on neck structures by the weight of the body
C) Closure of the nostrils and mouth at the same time from outside
D) Compression on the chest and abdomen by external force
E) Occlusion of the respiratory passages from inside
Correct Answer: C) Closure of the nostrils and mouth at the same time from outside
Explanation:
Smothering occurs at the level of the mouth and nose, involving external closure of both openings simultaneously, and is mostly suicidal though it can be homicidal. This differs from gagging, which produces a similar effect but from inside the mouth (e.g., a cloth pushed into the mouth and pharynx).
###
A wound characterized by irregular, tear-like edges caused by blunt trauma is best classified as which type of injury?
A) Incision
B) Abrasion
C) Contusion
D) Laceration
E) Puncture wound
Correct Answer: D) Laceration
Explanation:
Lacerations are irregular tear-like wounds caused by blunt trauma, requiring a firm structure (such as bone) beneath the skin to allow the tissue to tear and the skin to open. This contrasts with incisions, which have regular edges and clear margins from a sharp object, and abrasions, which are superficial scrapes of the epidermis only.
###
The color of skin discoloration seen in postmortem hypostasis is primarily determined by which factor?
A) The ambient temperature at the death scene
B) The position in which the body was found
C) The chemical state of hemoglobin molecules
D) The total blood volume remaining in the vessels
E) The time elapsed since cardiac arrest
Correct Answer: C) The chemical state of hemoglobin molecules
Explanation:
Postmortem hypostasis color depends on the chemical state of hemoglobin (e.g., carboxyhemoglobin, methemoglobin), which is why, for example, carbon monoxide poisoning classically produces a pink or light red discoloration rather than the usual bluish-purple color. This makes hypostasis color a useful clue for suspecting certain causes of death, such as CO poisoning.
