normal6/12, what does this mean?
A) They can see at 6 meters what a person with normal vision can see at 12 meters
B) They can see at 12 meters what a person with normal vision can see at 6 meters
C) Their vision is better than normal, resolving details invisible to the average eye at 6 meters
D) They have normal visual acuity equivalent to 6/6
E) They can only perceive hand movements at a distance of 6 meters
Correct Answer: A) They can see at 6 meters what a person with normal vision can see at 12 meters
Explanation:
Visual acuity is expressed as test distance/line read by a normal eye. A 6/12 result means the patient stands at the standard 6-meter testing distance but can only resolve letters that a normal-sighted person could read from 12 meters, indicating reduced acuity. Option B reverses the fraction and describes better-than-normal vision, which is incorrect.
